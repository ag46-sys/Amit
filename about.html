About Me

I am a Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bhubaneswar. My research focuses on renewable energy integration, hybrid microgrid energy management, synchornization of hetrogenous microgrid and grid-forming/grid-following converter control.

Over the past five years, I designed advanced control strategies for interconnection and stable operation of heterogeneous microgrids under balanced, unbalanced, and fault conditions. I have developed unified synchronization and adaptive droop control frameworks that enable cost-optimal power sharing, enhanced transient stability, and seamless integration of distributed energy resources. I have extensive hands-on experience in real-time simulation and experimental validation, including the development of a 20 kW prototype microgrid. I also have experience in Power Hardware-in-the-Loop (PHIL) testing using OPAL-RT.
